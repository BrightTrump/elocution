    • Diction Coaching

    While developing our clients on good diction, we focus on their Articulation, Pronunciation, Intonation, Pace, Voice quality and Context

    Articulation: Clear articulation is essential for effective diction. We encourage our students/clients to practice enunciating each syllable and sound clearly.

    Pronunciation: Correct pronunciation is crucial for conveying meaning and avoiding misunderstandings. Make sure students are pronouncing words correctly by using a dictionary or other reliable source.

    Intonation: The way in which words are stressed can affect their meaning and impact hence, we teach our students to pay attention to intonation patterns, including rising and falling intonation, and how they can be used to convey different emotions and attitudes.

    Pace: The pace at which words are spoken can also affect their impact. We teach and encourage our clients to vary their pace depending on the context and message they are trying to convey.

    Voice quality: The quality of a student's voice can affect how well their words are understood hence our focus on students to practice speaking with a clear and resonant voice.

    Context: Finally, we priortise teaching our students/clients to consider the context in which they are speaking. Different situations may call for different types of diction, such as formal or informal language. We always encourage our students to be aware of these differences and adjust their diction accordingly.
